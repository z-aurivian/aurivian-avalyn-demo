## 2. Pipeline
| Asset | What it is | Indication | Lead trial | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **AP01** | Inhaled pirfenidone | Progressive pulmonary fibrosis (PPF), incl. CTD-ILD (RA-ILD, SSc-ILD, HP) | **MIST** (Ph2b, NCT06329401) FVC @ wk52 | Enrollment (375 pts) completed June 2026; topline H2 2027 |
| **AP02** | Inhaled nintedanib | IPF, treatment-naive | **AURA-IPF** (Ph2, NCT07194382) FVC @ wk12 | First patient dosed Mar 2026; data late 2027 |
| **AP03** | Inhaled pirfenidone + nintedanib fixed-dose combo | TBD | — | Preclinical/IND-enabling; Ph1 planned later 2026 |

- Both AP01 and AP02 aim to replicate oral antifibrotic efficacy while sharply cutting systemic exposure (company PK claims: ~15–68x lower systemic exposure vs. oral; label these as company-reported, not independently verified).
- AP01 earlier Ph1b **ATLAS** trial (published *Thorax* 2023): 100mg BID showed near-stabilized FVC through 48 weeks vs. continued decline on lower dose — real dose-response signal, no placebo arm.
- Competitive differentiation is **route of administration**, not novel mechanism — reformulating proven oral antifibrotics for inhaled/lung-targeted delivery to reduce the GI/hepatic/dermatologic tolerability burden that drives discontinuation on Esbriet/Ofev.

## 3. Therapeutic area
- IPF: prevalence estimates vary widely by case definition (~30–90/100k). PPF: verified 70.3/100k (Olson et al. 2021). SSc-ILD: SSc affects ~1/6,500 adults, ILD is the #1 cause of SSc death (~33%).
- SoC: oral pirfenidone (Esbriet) + nintedanib (Ofev) for IPF; nintedanib alone approved for SSc-ILD (SENSCIS) and PPF (INBUILD). No pirfenidone approval in either. SSc-ILD first-line often MMF; tocilizumab (Actemra) also approved.
- Tolerability burden driving unmet need: pirfenidone discontinuation 14.6% vs 9.6% placebo (nausea 36%, rash 30%); nintedanib diarrhea 62–76%, discontinuation 21% vs 15% placebo.
- Newest oral competitor mechanism: Boehringer's **nerandomilast (Jascayd)**, PDE4B inhibitor, approved IPF Oct 2025 + PPF Dec 2025 — first new IPF mechanism in a decade.

## 6. Competitive landscape
- **United Therapeutics** — inhaled treprostinil (Tyvaso/Tyvaso DPI); **TETON-1/TETON-2** both met primary FVC endpoint in IPF (NEJM) — most advanced direct inhaled competitor, different drug class (prostacyclin, not antifibrotic).
- **Insmed** — TPIP (inhaled treprostinil palmitil), Ph2 PPF/IPF, Ph3 PH-ILD — earlier-stage inhaled competitor.
- **Boehringer Ingelheim** — Ofev (oral nintedanib) + newly approved nerandomilast/Jascayd (oral, new mechanism).
- **Roche/Genentech** — Esbriet facing generic erosion; novel biologic vixarelimab discontinued for futility (late 2025).
- **Bristol Myers Squibb** — admilparant (BMS-986278), oral LPA1 antagonist, Ph3 in both IPF and PPF.
- **Pliant Therapeutics** — bexotegrast IPF program **discontinued June 2025** (safety signal) — do not list as active.
- **FibroGen** — pamrevlumab, Ph3 failed 2023, dead in IPF.

**Bottom line for messaging**: Avalyn currently has no like-for-like inhaled-antifibrotic competitor — UT and Insmed are inhaled but a different drug class (prostacyclin). Its real competitive pressure is from oral drugs improving (nerandomilast) and from the adherence/tolerability narrative it's trying to win against Esbriet/Ofev incumbency.
