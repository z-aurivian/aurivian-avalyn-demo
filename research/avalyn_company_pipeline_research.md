## 1. Company & Pipeline Basics [INDEPENDENT FACT / COMPANY SOURCE]

Avalyn Pharma is a clinical-stage biotech developing inhaled reformulations of the two leading oral IPF antifibrotics, plus a fixed-dose inhaled combination:

- **AP01** — inhaled (reformulated) **pirfenidone** (vs. Roche/Genentech's oral Esbriet), delivered via the PARI eFlow nebulizer system. In Phase 2b ("MIST") trial in progressive pulmonary fibrosis (PPF); ~375-patient target enrollment; primary endpoint change in FVC at 52 weeks; topline data expected H2 2027. Phase 1b trial was called "ATLAS."
  Source: https://www.avalynpharma.com/pipeline/ ; https://pharmaphorum.com/news/avalyn-seeks-300m-its-ipo (Apr 30, 2026)
- **AP02** — inhaled (reformulated) **nintedanib** (vs. Boehringer Ingelheim's oral Ofev). Two Phase 1 studies completed; Phase 2 dose-selection study ("AURA-IPF," ~160 patients) in IPF, data expected late 2027.
  Source: same as above.
- **AP03** — fixed-dose inhaled **combination** of pirfenidone + nintedanib, in preclinical/IND-enabling work; Phase 1 entry planned later 2026.
  Source: same as above.
- Avalyn holds an exclusive license to PARI's eFlow Nebulizer System, which it frames (per aggregated search summaries, likely S-1/DRS-derived) as a durability advantage independent of oral-drug patent expiry. **[UNVERIFIED — could not independently fetch the SEC filing; sec.gov returned HTTP 403 to WebFetch]**
- Company completed a **$300M upsized IPO** (priced ~$18/share) around late April 2026; prior to that, raised **$100M Series D** (led by Suvretta Capital Management and SR One, with Novo Holdings among ~16 investors) and a **$175M Series C** (Sept 27, 2023, per GlobeNewswire press release). Total VC raised across four rounds reported as "over $370M," with ~$138M cash at end of 2025.
  Source: https://www.biopharmadive.com/news/avalyn-pharma-ipo-idiopathic-pulmonary-fibrosis/817072/ (BioPharma Dive, Apr 9, 2026, by Gwendolyn Wu); https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2023/09/27/2750197/... (Sept 27, 2023)

**Note on a caught extraction error:** one automated summary of an April 29, 2026 BioPharma Dive article initially mislabeled AP01 as inhaled Ofev — this is contradicted by Avalyn's own pipeline page and by Endpoints News and the April 9, 2026 BioPharma Dive article, which all agree AP01 = inhaled pirfenidone, AP02 = inhaled nintedanib. Treat the mislabeling as a one-off research error, not fact.

## 2. Avalyn's Own Stated Rationale for Inhaled vs. Oral Delivery — ALL COMPANY CLAIMS

These are Avalyn's own words (website, press releases, or company statements relayed by journalists) — **not independently verified pharmacology**, though several rest on Avalyn's own (unpublished/not-independently-audited) trial data:

- **Company website** (https://www.avalynpharma.com/pipeline/, undated):
  - "Safety and toxicity often limit the use of oral pirfenidone. Side effects are usually managed by dose reductions, which in turn can decrease the effectiveness of the drug."
  - Oral nintedanib "associated with side effects and toxicities, including diarrhea, hyperbilirubinemia, and drug-induced liver injury."
  - Inhaled delivery "targets the lungs directly with smaller doses than oral formulation, maximizing [drug] effect on diseased lung tissue while minimizing exposure to other tissues."
  - "AP01 was well tolerated with fewer systemic adverse events compared to historic oral pirfenidone" (cross-trial comparison, not head-to-head).
  - AP03 rationale: oral combination of pirfenidone + nintedanib "has not been feasible due to their additive side effect profiles"; inhaled delivery is positioned as the solution.

- **Series C press release** (GlobeNewswire, Sept 27, 2023): https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2023/09/27/2750197/0/en/Avalyn-Raises-175-Million-in-Oversubscribed-Series-C-Financing-to-Advance-Inhaled-Pulmonary-Fibrosis-Programs-into-Later-Stage-Clinical-Studies.html
  - CEO Lyn Baranowski: "There is an urgent need for effective treatments that patients can tolerate, which we believe can be addressed with inhaled delivery."
  - "While oral pirfenidone has been shown to reduce decline of lung function and mortality in patients with idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis (IPF), systemic exposure contributes to significant side effects including nausea, rash, photosensitivity, weight loss, and fatigue, limiting its overall use in patients."
  - "In clinical trials to date, inhaled AP01 has been well-tolerated and demonstrated enhanced efficacy, when compared to published results of oral pirfenidone" (cross-trial, not head-to-head).
  - Third-party investor quote (Dr. Gianna Hoffman-Luca, Perceptive Xontogeny), published in Avalyn's release: AP01 data show "preserved forced vital capacity in pulmonary fibrosis and significantly fewer side effects than oral pirfenidone."

- **Phase 1b ATLAS results release** (GlobeNewswire, Mar 23, 2023): https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2023/03/23/2633557/0/en/Avalyn-Pharma-Publishes-Phase-1b-ATLAS-Results-Demonstrating-Stabilization-of-Lung-Function-at-24-and-48-Weeks-with-AP01-and-Favorable-Safety-Profile.html
  - CEO Baranowski: "While oral pirfenidone has the potential to improve lung function by reducing fibrosis, its utility is limited by its poor tolerability profile." "AP01 has the potential to meet or exceed the efficacy of oral pirfenidone without the systemic toxicities that limit its adoption."
  - Independent clinician quote published by Avalyn (Dr. Alex West, Guy's and St Thomas' Hospital ILD lead): "AP01 has the potential to keep patients on effective therapy longer, which might translate into increased life expectancy and quality of life." (Third-party clinical opinion, but distributed via Avalyn's own release — not an independently published/peer-reviewed statement.)
  - Cited oral pirfenidone side effects: "elevated liver enzymes, diarrhea, vomiting and nausea." AP01 (company trial data): "well tolerated with no adverse effects on respiratory rate, spirometry, or oxygenation during or following administration."

- **Series D press release** (BioSpace, dated ~July 2025): https://www.biospace.com/press-releases/avalyn-completes-oversubscribed-100-million-series-d-financing-to-further-advance-clinical-development-of-novel-inhaled-therapies-for-pulmonary-fibrosis

  - Approach designed to "reduce systemic exposure and deliver medication directly to the site of disease."

- **BioPharma Dive coverage of IPO** (Apr 29, 2026, Gwendolyn Wu): https://www.biopharmadive.com/news/avalyn-biotech-ipo-price-pulmonary-fibrosis-esbriet-ofev/818851/

## 5. Independent, Factual Grounding: FDA Label Data for Esbriet and Ofev

This section is entirely independent regulatory/clinical data — **no Avalyn claims** — for factual competitive context.

### Pirfenidone (Esbriet) — from FDA-approved prescribing information
Primary source: https://www.gene.com/download/pdf/esbriet_prescribing.pdf (Genentech-hosted official FDA label; verified by direct PDF extraction)

- **Overall discontinuation due to adverse events**: 14.6% (Esbriet, 2403 mg/day) vs. 9.6% (placebo). Most common AEs leading to discontinuation: rash and nausea. (Sec. 6.1)
- **GI dose reduction/interruption**: required in 18.5% (Esbriet) vs. 5.8% (placebo); GI-related discontinuation 2.2% vs. 1.0%. (Sec. 5.4)
- **GI adverse event incidence** (pooled Phase 3 studies, N=623 pirfenidone vs. N=624 placebo): Nausea 36% vs. 16%; Diarrhea 26% vs. 20%; Dyspepsia 19% vs. 7%; Vomiting 13% vs. 6%; GERD 11% vs. 7%; Abdominal pain 24% vs. 15%. (Table 2)
- **Rash**: 30% vs. 10%.
- **Photosensitivity reaction**: 9% (Esbriet) vs. 1% (placebo); majority occurred in first 6 months.
- **Severe cutaneous adverse reactions** (SJS, TEN, DRESS) reported post-marketing.
- **Liver enzyme elevation** (ALT/AST ≥3x ULN): 3.7% (Esbriet) vs. 0.8% (placebo); ≥10x ULN: 0.3% vs. 0.2%. Postmarketing reports include severe liver injury with fatal outcome. Label requires LFTs before initiation, monthly for 6 months, then every 3 months.
- **Other AEs (5–<10%)**: pruritus 8% vs. 5%; asthenia 6% vs. 4%; dysgeusia 6% vs. 2%; non-cardiac chest pain 5% vs. 4%.
- **Time course**: GI events highest in first 3 months, decreasing thereafter.
- A WebSearch AI-summary claim of "discontinuation rates as high as 30% in some analyses" **could not be verified against any specific source** — not to be used; the only confirmed overall discontinuation figure is 14.6% vs. 9.6% from the FDA label.

### Nintedanib (Ofev) — from FDA-approved prescribing information (via DailyMed/NIH) and peer-reviewed literature
Primary source: DailyMed (NIH repository of official FDA labeling) https://dailymed.nlm.nih.gov/dailymed/drugInfo.cfm?setid=da1c9f37-779e-4682-816f-93d0faa4cfc9 (accessdata.fda.gov PDF returned 404/bot-block, so DailyMed was used as the authoritative label mirror)

- **Diarrhea**: IPF trials 62% (Ofev) vs. 18% (placebo) — dose reduction 11% vs. 0%, discontinuation 5% vs. <1%. Chronic fibrosing ILD: 67% vs. 24%. SSc-ILD: 76% vs. 32%.
- **Nausea/vomiting** (IPF): nausea 24% vs. 7%; vomiting 12% vs. 3%. (SSc-ILD: nausea 32% vs. 14%; vomiting 25% vs. 10%.)
- **Liver enzyme elevation**: IPF 14% (Ofev) vs. 3% (placebo); SSc-ILD 13% vs. 3%.
- **Weight decreased**: IPF 10% vs. 3%; SSc-ILD 12% vs. 4%.
- **Bleeding**: IPF 10% (Ofev) vs. 7% (placebo), epistaxis most common; postmarketing reports include fatal bleeding events.
- **Overall AE-driven discontinuation**: IPF 21% (Ofev) vs. 15% (placebo); chronic fibrosing ILD 20% vs. 10%; SSc-ILD 16% vs. 9%. Most frequent discontinuation causes: diarrhea (5%), nausea (2%), decreased appetite (2%).
- **GI perforation warning**: label warns Ofev may increase GI perforation risk (<1% in IPF trials vs. 0 placebo); postmarketing fatal cases reported.
- **Hepatotoxicity monitoring**: LFTs recommended before initiation, at regular intervals for first 3 months, periodically thereafter; dose reduction/discontinuation guidance based on transaminase thresholds.

Corroborating peer-reviewed sources:
- **NIH LiverTox** (https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK548135/): serum enzyme elevations in 8–16% of nintedanib patients vs. 3% controls; severe elevations (>3x ULN) 3–5% vs. <1%; some pharmacovigilance datasets report ALT/AST elevation as high as 68% (usually self-limited); LiverTox causality score "C" (probable cause of clinically apparent liver injury that can be severe/fatal).
- **INBUILD trial safety analysis** (Cottin V, Martinez FJ, Jenkins RG, et al., Respiratory Research 2022, https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8991727/): diarrhea 72.3% (nintedanib) vs. 25.7% (placebo); diarrhea-driven discontinuation 6.3% vs. 0.3%; overall AE discontinuation 22.0% vs. 14.5%; liver enzymes ≥3x ULN 14.2% vs. 1.8%; weight decreased 14.8% vs. 5.4%.
- **Real-world tolerability study** (Kato M, Sasaki S, Nakamura T, et al., Scientific Reports 2019, https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6700168/): grade ≥2 nausea in 32.5% (16.8% required discontinuation); grade ≥2 diarrhea in 35.1% (12.9% required discontinuation); low BMI identified as a significant risk factor.
